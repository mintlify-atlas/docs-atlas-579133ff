title: "World generation: text and image to 3D scenes"
description: "A four-stage pipeline — HY-Pano 2.0, WorldNav, WorldStereo 2.0, and WorldMirror 2.0 — converts a text prompt or single image into a navigable 3D world."
---

World generation is the synthesis half of HY-World 2.0. Starting from nothing more than a text description or a single photograph, it produces a fully navigable 3D world saved as 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) or mesh assets — not a video clip. Unlike world reconstruction, which derives 3D structure from existing multi-view photos or footage, world generation synthesizes new geometry and appearance entirely from scratch, then composes it into a persistent scene you can explore in Blender, Unity, Unreal Engine, or Isaac Sim.

## The four-stage pipeline

World generation runs through four sequential stages, each handled by a dedicated model component.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Panorama generation — HY-Pano 2.0">
    An MMDiT-based model converts a text prompt or a single-view image into a 360° equirectangular panorama. This panorama gives the downstream stages a complete view of the scene's appearance and layout before any 3D structure is computed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Trajectory planning — WorldNav">
    WorldNav analyses the panorama and plans a physically plausible camera trajectory through the scene. The trajectory defines the viewpoints that WorldStereo 2.0 will render, ensuring the final world is fully explorable from a variety of angles.
  </Step>
  <Step title="World expansion — WorldStereo 2.0">
    A memory-driven video generation model takes the panorama and the planned camera trajectory and renders a consistent multi-view sequence. A persistent memory buffer keeps appearance and geometry coherent as the virtual camera moves through the scene.
  </Step>
  <Step title="World composition — WorldMirror 2.0 + 3DGS">
    WorldMirror 2.0 lifts the multi-view frames into a dense point cloud with camera parameters. A 3DGS optimization step then fits Gaussian primitives — means, scales, rotations, opacities, and spherical harmonics — to the point cloud, producing the final real-time-renderable 3D world.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Open-source status

<Note>
  WorldMirror 2.0 — the world composition stage — is open-source today. HY-Pano 2.0, WorldNav, and WorldStereo 2.0 are still pending release. As interim alternatives, use [HunyuanWorld 1.0](https://github.com/Tencent-Hunyuan/HunyuanWorld-1.0) for panorama generation and [WorldStereo](https://github.com/FuchengSu/WorldStereo) for world expansion.
</Note>
